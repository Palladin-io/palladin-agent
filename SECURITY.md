# Security Policy

Palladin is a password manager. Please do not report security vulnerabilities through public GitHub issues, discussions, or pull requests.

## Reporting a vulnerability

Email **patryk.roguszewski@palladin.io** with `Security` in the subject, or follow the instructions at [palladin.io/.well-known/security.txt](https://palladin.io/.well-known/security.txt).

Include:

- a description of the vulnerability and its potential impact,
- the affected version, commit, or component,
- minimal, safe reproduction steps,
- your contact details if you want a response.

Do not send acquired secrets or personal data. An anonymized proof is sufficient.

## Safe harbor and testing boundaries

Good-faith research limited to the minimum necessary test on accounts and data you own, or in an environment you are expressly authorized to test, will not be treated as a violation of Palladin's vulnerability-testing restrictions.

The full disclosure process, response targets, scope, and safe-harbor conditions are defined in Palladin's [Vulnerability Disclosure Policy](https://palladin.io/vulnerability-disclosure).

Do not:

- access or modify another person's data,
- perform social engineering,
- disrupt availability or destroy data,
- retain or disclose secrets,
- publicly disclose a vulnerability before Palladin has had a reasonable opportunity to investigate and remediate it.

We will acknowledge a report and coordinate remediation and disclosure directly with the reporter.
